I am a botanist and evolutionary biologist who uses collections data and computational tools to understand the origins and evolution of plant diversity. My research spans the fields of plant systematics, phylogenomics, paleobotany, biogeography, and biodiversity informatics.

A central goal of my research is to better understand the processes that underlie phenotypic innovation and trait evolution in plants at both shallow and deep evolutionary scales. I also am broadly interested in how phylogeny and geologic history (including climate change) intersect to shape the geographic distributions of plants and the assembly of ancient and modern plant communities.

I am a postdoc at the Kunming Institute of Botany (CAS), and a research associate at the National Museum of Natural History (SI). Previously I was a postdoc at in the EEB Department at the University of Michigan. I received my PhD in Botany from the University of Florida.
